# AquaFlow Systems Inc. — Company Profile

## Overview

AquaFlow Systems Inc. is a Denver, Colorado-based manufacturer and servicer of industrial water treatment and filtration equipment. Founded in 1998, the company serves municipal water authorities, industrial facilities, and commercial real estate operators across 38 U.S. states and three Canadian provinces.

AquaFlow's core product lines include membrane filtration systems, UV disinfection units, reverse osmosis assemblies, and PFAS remediation systems. The company generates revenue through equipment sales, long-term service contracts (typically 3–7 years), and recurring consumable sales (filter media, UV lamps, chemical dosing).

## Business Model

AquaFlow's recurring revenue engine is its service contract portfolio. As of Q1 2024, the company holds 847 active service contracts with weighted average remaining term of 4.1 years. Service contract customers exhibit 91% renewal rates and 8% annual revenue expansion from upsells.

The company's installed base of approximately 14,200 units in the field creates a captive aftermarket for consumables and service. This installed-base dynamic produces roughly 59% of revenue from predictable recurring streams, which compresses earnings volatility and supports favorable debt service coverage.

## Key Customers

AquaFlow's top-10 customers represent 38% of FY2023 revenue. The most significant single customer is the Denver Metro Water Authority (DMWA), whose $19.4M annual service contract represents 6.2% of LTM revenue. The DMWA contract expires December 31, 2024 and is subject to competitive re-bid.

Other notable customers include industrial facilities in the energy sector (refinery wastewater treatment), food and beverage manufacturers (process water purification), and municipal water utilities across the Mountain West and Pacific Northwest.

## Products and Technology

**Membrane Systems (HydraFlo Series):** Ultrafiltration and nanofiltration membranes for municipal and industrial applications. The company holds 14 active U.S. patents covering membrane architecture and automated backwash sequencing.

**UV Disinfection (ClearWave Series):** Validated against EPA UVDGM and NSF/ANSI 55 standards; primary market is drinking water treatment for utilities.

**PFAS Remediation (PurePath Series):** Launched in 2022, targeting the rapidly growing market for per- and polyfluoroalkyl substance treatment in groundwater, municipal supply, and industrial effluent. Three provisional patents filed Q1 2024 on next-generation ion exchange media.

**Remote Monitoring (AquaView IoT Platform):** Cloud-based telemetry for 78% of the installed base; drives predictive maintenance scheduling and contract upsells.

## Competitive Position

The water treatment equipment market is moderately fragmented. AquaFlow's primary competitors include Hydra Solutions Corp. (privately held, est. revenue $520M, the market leader in the Mountain West), Xylem Inc. (NYSE: XYL, large-cap), Veolia Water Technologies (subsidiary of Veolia Environnement), and several regional service providers.

AquaFlow's competitive differentiation rests on:
- Depth of service network (22 regional service centers, 318 field technicians)
- AquaView IoT platform integration driving retention
- PFAS remediation capability at a time of heightened EPA regulatory attention
- Strong relationships with municipal procurement officers in core geographies

## Management Team

The management team has demonstrated consistent mid-single-digit organic revenue growth and EBITDA margin expansion of ~200 bps over the past three years through pricing discipline and service mix improvement.

## Growth Strategy

AquaFlow's management has articulated a three-pronged growth plan:
1. **Geographic expansion:** Penetrate the Southeast and Mid-Atlantic markets where the company currently has minimal installed base but high PFAS remediation demand
2. **Platform upsell:** Migrate legacy service customers to AquaView IoT-enabled service plans at 20–30% premium
3. **PFAS-driven new contracts:** Respond to EPA PFAS maximum contaminant level rulemakings driving mandatory treatment upgrades at ~2,400 utilities nationwide

Management projects the organic revenue CAGR at 7–9% through 2027 under base-case assumptions, with EBITDA margin expanding to 26–28% as service mix improves and SG&A leverage is achieved.

## Regulatory Environment

EPA's National Primary Drinking Water Regulation for PFOA and PFAS (April 2024) is expected to be a significant demand driver. An estimated 2,400 community water systems must install treatment by 2029. AquaFlow's PurePath series positions the company to capture a meaningful portion of this $4–6B addressable remediation capital cycle.

Additional regulatory tailwinds include the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA) water infrastructure allocations and state-level PFAS regulations in California, Michigan, and New York that exceed federal minimums.
