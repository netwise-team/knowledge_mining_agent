# Water Treatment Equipment Market — Industry Analysis

## Market Size and Growth

The U.S. water treatment equipment market was valued at approximately $18.7B in 2023 and is projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate of 6.8% through 2030, reaching an estimated $29.4B. Global market size stands at $68B with Asia-Pacific representing the fastest-growing region due to urbanization and industrial water demand.

Market drivers include aging municipal infrastructure (EPA estimates $625B in water infrastructure investment needed over 20 years), tightening federal and state water quality regulations, industrial water reuse mandates, and the rapidly expanding PFAS remediation opportunity.

## Competitive Landscape

The market is moderately fragmented at the equipment level but concentrated at the large-scale EPC (engineering, procurement, construction) tier. Key participants by segment:

**Large-Cap Integrated Water Companies:**
- Xylem Inc. (NYSE: XYL) — revenue ~$7.4B, globally diversified, strong in analytics and digital water
- Veolia Water Technologies — subsidiary of Veolia Environnement (ENXTPA: VIE), European-led, strong in industrial and municipal
- SUEZ Water Technologies — subsidiary post-2021 Veolia-SUEZ merger, rebranded as Veolia business unit

**Mid-Market Equipment Specialists:**
- Hydra Solutions Corp. — privately held, est. ~$520M revenue, dominant Mountain West municipal market
- Evoqua Water Technologies — acquired by Xylem in 2023 for $7.5B, formerly NYSE: AQUA
- RODI Systems — privately held, industrial and commercial focus, Southeast U.S.

**Regional Service Providers:**
- Numerous regional operators with $5–50M revenue; primarily service and parts, limited new equipment capability

## Market Segmentation

By end-market:
- Municipal Water Utilities: 44% of market (driven by regulation and infrastructure spending)
- Industrial (Manufacturing, Food & Beverage, Energy): 31%
- Commercial Real Estate and Institutional: 14%
- Agriculture and Irrigation: 11%

By product type:
- Filtration Systems (membrane, sand, activated carbon): 38%
- Disinfection (UV, chlorination, ozone): 19%
- Softening and Demineralization: 14%
- PFAS and Emerging Contaminant Treatment: 9% (fastest growing, from <2% in 2019)
- Service and Parts: 20%

## PFAS Remediation Sub-Market

Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) represent the fastest-growing opportunity in water treatment. EPA's April 2024 PFAS NPDWR set maximum contaminant levels (MCLs) for PFOA, PFOS, and four other PFAS compounds at 4 parts per trillion — requiring treatment upgrades at an estimated 2,400 community water systems.

Total U.S. PFAS treatment capital deployment is estimated at $4–6B over the 2024–2029 compliance window. Primary treatment technologies are granular activated carbon (GAC), anion exchange resin (AER), and nanofiltration/reverse osmosis. Companies with strong PFAS treatment product lines and utility relationships are positioned to capture disproportionate share.

State-level regulation further accelerates demand: California, Massachusetts, Michigan, and New York have adopted PFAS MCLs below federal levels, driving earlier compliance timelines.

## Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act Impact

The IIJA (November 2021) allocated $55B to water infrastructure over five years, including:
- $15B to replace lead service lines
- $10B for PFAS and emerging contaminant treatment grants and loans
- $23B for Clean Water and Drinking Water State Revolving Fund (SRF) capitalization
- $7B for water reuse, recycling, and water storage

Municipal budgets constrained during COVID-19 are now being supplemented by IIJA disbursements, creating a multi-year tailwind for capital equipment procurement. Equipment vendors with established state SRF program relationships and compliant procurement documentation are advantaged.

## Pricing Dynamics and Margin Trends

Equipment gross margins range from 35–45% for standard product lines, with customized or engineered-to-order systems achieving 42–55%. Service contract gross margins are typically 50–65% given the labor-and-parts model and low capital intensity once installed.

Inflation in 2021–2023 pressured raw material costs (stainless steel, specialty membranes, UV lamps), but leading vendors largely recovered margin through contract price escalators and surcharges. Membrane prices have moderated in 2024 following supply chain normalization, providing a tailwind to equipment margins.

## Key Demand Drivers

1. **EPA PFAS rulemaking:** Single largest near-term demand catalyst; 2,400 utilities must comply by 2029.
2. **Lead service line replacement:** IIJA funding driving parallel water quality investments.
3. **Industrial water reuse:** Drought conditions and water cost pressure accelerating industrial recycle/reuse projects, particularly in Southwest U.S. manufacturing.
4. **IoT and digital water:** Utilities and industrials seeking real-time monitoring and predictive maintenance; vendors with integrated telemetry platforms command service premium.
5. **Climate-driven supply risk:** Drought reducing surface water availability increases reliance on groundwater (higher treatment needs) and desalination.

## M&A Activity

The water treatment sector has seen consistent consolidation. Notable recent transactions:
- Xylem / Evoqua: $7.5B (2023) — the largest U.S. water sector M&A in recent history; created a $7B+ revenue leader
- Veolia / SUEZ: ~$15B global transaction (2021) — created a European water mega-player
- Several private equity-backed platform acquisitions at 8–12x EBITDA for mid-market specialists

Typical M&A multiples for mid-market equipment and service companies range from 7.5–11x LTM EBITDA for quality assets with recurring revenue. Businesses with strong service contract portfolios, technology differentiation (IoT, PFAS), and limited customer concentration trade at the high end of the range.

## Outlook

The water treatment equipment market is structurally positioned for above-GDP growth through the decade driven by (1) regulatory compliance cycles, (2) IIJA capital deployment, (3) PFAS treatment build-out, and (4) industrial water reuse acceleration. The IoT-enabled service model is becoming a competitive requirement rather than a differentiator, as utilities demand predictive maintenance capabilities and remote monitoring.

Vendors with broad geographic service coverage, PFAS treatment capability, and integrated digital platforms are best positioned to compound market growth with share gains and service revenue expansion.
