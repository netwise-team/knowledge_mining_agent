# Legal Due Diligence — Process and Checklist

## Purpose of Legal Due Diligence

Legal due diligence (legal DD) is a systematic review of a target company's legal affairs conducted by the buyer's legal counsel before completing an acquisition. Its objectives are:
1. Identify contractual, regulatory, or litigation risks that affect deal value or deal structure
2. Confirm the seller can transfer the assets or shares being sold without encumbrance
3. Verify representations and warranties in the purchase agreement are accurate
4. Identify items requiring indemnification, escrow, or purchase price adjustment

## Scope Areas in Legal DD

### 1. Corporate and Governance Review

- Corporate formation documents (certificate of incorporation, bylaws, operating agreements)
- Capitalization table: confirm authorized/issued shares, options, warrants, convertible notes
- Board and shareholder resolutions: authorization for the transaction
- Subsidiary structure: confirm all subsidiaries identified
- Operating agreements/JV agreements: confirm no change-of-control consent rights held by third parties
- Related-party transactions: any transactions with owners, directors, affiliates at non-market terms

**Red flag:** Change-of-control provisions in key agreements that require third-party consent — can block or delay close.

### 2. Material Contracts Review

Material contracts include revenue contracts (top-10 customers; any contract >5% of revenue), key supply agreements, real property leases, equipment financing, employment agreements for key management, and non-compete agreements.

Legal counsel reviews each material contract for:
- Change-of-control provisions (does a sale trigger consent requirements?)
- Termination rights (can the counterparty terminate on change of ownership?)
- Assignment restrictions (can the contract be transferred to the new entity?)
- Pricing terms and renewal options

**Rule of thumb:** Any contract with >$500K annual value receives full legal review; contracts >$5M or >5% of revenue receive priority.

### 3. Intellectual Property

**Patents:**
- U.S. Patent Office search to confirm registration, ownership, and status
- Review for co-ownership or encumbrances
- Inter Partes Review (IPR) proceedings or validity challenges pending
- Patent expiration schedule (expirations on core products within hold period are material)

**Trademarks and Trade Dress:**
- Registered U.S. and international trademarks
- Domain name registration

**Trade Secrets and Proprietary Know-How:**
- Employee confidentiality agreements (are all key employees bound?)
- Work-for-hire agreements with all contractors
- Assignment of inventions by former employees
- Open-source software usage audit (GPL contamination risk in commercial products)

### 4. Litigation and Disputes

Legal counsel reviews pending and threatened litigation (civil, arbitration, regulatory), settled matters within the past 3–5 years, OSHA investigations and citations, EPA/regulatory enforcement actions, and customer warranty claims.

For each pending matter, counsel assesses:
- Status in proceedings
- Estimated exposure (best case, worst case, likely outcome)
- Insurance coverage and deductible/coverage gaps
- Likelihood of adverse outcome and timeline

### 5. Regulatory Compliance

**Environmental:**
- Phase I ESA review (conducted by environmental consultant)
- RCRA, TSCA, CERCLA compliance
- Permits: air quality, water discharge (NPDES), hazardous waste
- PFAS exposure screen (manufacturing use; proximate contamination)

**Employment:**
- FLSA (Fair Labor Standards Act) compliance: exempt vs. non-exempt classification
- ERISA: pension plan status, funding obligations, multiemployer plan exposure
- I-9 compliance and immigration status of workforce
- Pending NLRB charges or unionization efforts
- State-specific employment regulations (California WARN Act, pay equity laws)

**Industry-Specific (Water Treatment):**
EPA Safe Drinking Water Act compliance, state environmental agency permits, NSF/ANSI certifications for products used in drinking water applications.

### 6. Real Property

- Title searches on owned properties
- Phase I/II ESA on all owned properties
- Lease review: rent terms, landlord consent rights, renewal options, assignment provisions
- Zoning compliance for current use

### 7. Debt and Finance

- Outstanding indebtedness schedule
- Credit facility review: prepayment provisions, consent rights, change-of-control triggers
- Security interests (UCC filings): confirm all secured creditors identified
- Off-balance-sheet arrangements: operating leases, purchase commitments, earn-outs

## Key Legal Structuring Issues in LBO Transactions

**Change-of-Control Consent:** If key customer contracts require consent on change of control, the deal timeline must accommodate the consent process.

**Escrow and Indemnification:**
Where legal DD identifies uncertain but quantifiable exposures (pending litigation, environmental, warranty claims), the buyer typically requires:
- A portion of the purchase price held in escrow for 12–24 months
- Seller indemnification for losses exceeding the deductible and up to the indemnification cap
- Representations and Warranty Insurance (RWI) covering unknown breaches of seller reps/warranties

**Representations and Warranty Insurance (RWI):**
RWI has become standard in PE transactions >$50M. It shifts the risk of unknown rep/warranty breaches from the seller to an insurer, allowing sellers to make clean exits. Premium is typically 3–4% of policy limits; buyer pays the premium in most cases. RWI excludes known issues (carved out), fraud, forward-looking statements, and certain categories (PFAS, FCPA in high-risk jurisdictions).

## Typical Legal DD Timeline

| Week | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 1–2 | Organize data room; populate legal DD request list |
| 2–4 | Review corporate documents, key contracts, IP, litigation |
| 3–5 | Follow-up management interviews; legal expert calls |
| 4–6 | Draft legal DD report; identify issues for purchase agreement negotiation |
| 6–8 | Finalize legal DD; negotiate reps/warranties; finalize escrow/indemnification |

Accelerated timelines (4–5 weeks) are achievable for smaller or less complex deals; larger multi-jurisdictional transactions may require 10–12 weeks.
